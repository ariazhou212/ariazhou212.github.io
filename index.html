01. Discord Pro

2021.10

App Redesign

for Social Anxiety Disorder Players

Challenge

Social media were designed to help people connect. However, users feel more separated with current social media platforms. By focusing on people with Social Anxiety Disorder, I want to explore how might we help the gaming community have more desirable and less anxious ways of communication.

Social Anxiety Disorder Players

Secondary Research & Interviews

Behavioral archetype

Discord Pro

This archetype is mostly introverted people. They may feel uncomfortable talking to people in person, so the cyber world is a relatively secure place to make connections and relax.
They prefer to play with the same people for a long time due to the anxiety of talking to strangers. In a multiplayer game, they may blame themselves for any bad performance that possibly leads to defeat. They’re more sensitive to others’ criticism as well.
Although they have mental disabilities, they want to feel understood by others and need connections more than normal people. They need others’ approval to feel positive. However, it takes a longer time for them to trust others and learn how to cooperate with other teammates.

Key Takeaways & Future Steps

Pitch it to Discord?

This is a school project. We initially chose to redesign Discord for the multiplayer online battle games. But as we conducted research, we noticed that the criticisms are mostly about privacy issues. So we decided to focus on the minority group of SAD players and tried to make it as inclusive as possible.

We did a few iterations on the card design - our essential feature. But we don’t have the time and resources to test them. We also developed a design system for the card, but we still need to figure out whether it’s feasible to apply to other elements in the app.

Coffee Industry in China

An Emerging Market

6th

Market size

Tea remains the traditional drink of China. The tendency of Chinese consumers to prefer tea over coffee ranks the Chinese coffee market only at the 6th largest coffee market in the world. China’s consumption of coffee per capita remains relatively low at around 7 cups a year.

Chinese coffee consumption has risen spectacularly with a 500% increase between 2006 and 2018. According to the data from the China Coffee Association Beijing (CCAB) coffee consumption will increase at an annual rate of 15%.

15%

Middle-class young adults

The young people of the emerging middle class represent the driving force (75%). Within this segment, female customers represent the largest share-70%. Young consumers are demanding and pay particular attention to professionalism, services tailored to their tastes and the diversification of menus among the various coffees.
